About Me

Hi, my name is Arijit Paul. I am currently a fourth-year PhD candidate at Indiana University (IU) Bloomington. My advisor is Dylan Thurston (currently at Boston College). Before coming to IU, I completed an integrated BS-MS with a specialization in Mathematics from Indian Institute of Science Education and Research (IISER) Pune, India.

My mathematical interests mostly lie in the realm of low-dimensional topology. More specifically, I am interested in homological invariants of knots, links and three and four manifolds. I have spent a good amount of time exploring avenues in bordered sutured Floer homology, Khovanov homology and related invariants for three and four manifolds. More recently, I have started dipping my toes in the rivers of finite-type invariants. I also have a passing interest in quantum topology and topological quantum field theories (TQFTs).

In my free time, you can find me doodling, sketching and painting random little things, obsessing about all things Pokémon, trying to get back into reading books and enjoying board games and (occasionally) TTRPGs.
